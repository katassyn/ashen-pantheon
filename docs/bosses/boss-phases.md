# Boss Phases

All major bosses in Ashen Pantheon use a **multi-phase fight system**. As a boss's health drops, the encounter changes — new mechanics appear, existing ones become more dangerous, and the visual presentation escalates.

## Why Phases Matter

A single-phase boss fight is a test of one skill set. A multi-phase boss fight is a test of adaptability. Ashen Pantheon bosses are designed so that the strategy that works at 100% health does not simply work at 20% health — you must adjust.

## Standard Phase Structure

Most bosses use a three-phase structure, though this varies:

| Phase | HP Threshold | Typical Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | 100% – 66% | Core moveset, learning phase, moderate difficulty |
| Phase 2 | 66% – 33% | New abilities added, arena may change, damage increases |
| Phase 3 | 33% – 0% | Enraged state, highest damage, signature ultimate ability |

## Phase Transitions

Phase transitions are dramatic, scripted moments:

- Boss becomes temporarily invulnerable
- A cutscene or animation plays showing the boss's escalation
- The arena may change (new hazards appear, geometry shifts)
- The boss resets any stacks or statuses

Phase transitions are the safest moment to use consumables and communicate with your party.

## Enrage Timers

Some bosses have a soft enrage — if you do not reach a phase threshold within a time limit, the boss gains a permanent damage buff. This prevents excessively slow fights.

## Phase-Specific Mechanics

Many bosses have mechanics unique to a single phase:

- A Greek boss might summon divine champions only in Phase 2
- A Norse boss might trigger Ragnarok effects in Phase 3, filling the arena with rising water
- An Egyptian boss might require you to solve a puzzle during the Phase 1→2 transition
