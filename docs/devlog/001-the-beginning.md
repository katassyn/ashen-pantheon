# #001 — The Beginning

*April 17, 2026*

---

Every project starts somewhere. This one started with a frustration.

I've played a lot of action RPGs. I love the genre deeply — the satisfying click of a build coming together, the moment a boss's pattern finally clicks, the way a well-designed skill tree can hold your attention for hundreds of hours. But I kept running into the same walls: instancing systems that felt like afterthoughts, boss fights that were just stat checks dressed up in flashy animations, and ascendancy systems that felt like the designers ran out of budget halfway through.

So I started designing the game I wanted to play.

## What Ashen Pantheon Is

At its core, it's a **top-down 2D action RPG** — responsive, fast, skill-expressive. The camera sits above the character. Combat is immediate. Every fight is something you actively participate in, not something you watch your character do.

The instancing is first-class. Your world is yours. That means no competition for resources, no one ruining your boss attempt, no waiting for a respawn. But it doesn't mean isolation — the global layer keeps you connected to everyone else: chat is shared, rankings are live, trading works across instances.

The bosses are mythological. Not loosely inspired — actually pulled from real pantheons. Greek, Norse, Egyptian, Japanese, Slavic. I've been doing a lot of reading.

## The Three Classes

Three base classes: **Warrior**, **Mage**, **Archer**. Clean archetypes, but with room to go strange. Each branches into three ascendancies at level 50 — nine total paths, each with a distinct mechanical identity. The Hexblade is a chaos-DoT mage who curses targets and watches them rot. The Shadowstalker is an archer who closes to melee range and backstabs. The Pathfinder is a tank archer who wins by outlasting everything.

## The Two-Phase Skill System

Levels 1–50: broad skill tree, many nodes, class-specific paths, deep customization. Levels 50–100: focused ascendancy tree, 25 powerful points, 1 per 2 levels. The inflection at 50 is the most important decision in the game.

## Why This Wiki

I'm building this wiki as a public development document — it will grow as the game grows. Pages marked **[TODO]** aren't placeholders forever; they're honest signals about where development hasn't reached yet. When a skill tree gets designed, the wiki gets updated. When a boss gets named, the wiki gets its page.

This is a living document.
