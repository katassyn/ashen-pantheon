# DEVLOG #001 — Why I'm Building Ashen Pantheon

*April 17, 2026 · by Katassyn*

---

Hey. I'm Maks, 22, CS student from Poland. And I'm building an MMO RPG.

Not modding one. Not reskinning one. Building one.

This idea didn't come out of nowhere. For years I played games like Drakensang Online, Path of Exile, Metin2 — games where you sink hundreds of hours into a character, grind gear, master builds, fight bosses that actually feel like bosses. That feeling of progression, of your character becoming something powerful through your decisions — I've been chasing that my whole life as a player.

So at some point I stopped asking "why doesn't anyone make the game I want" and started trying to make it myself.

My first real attempt was a Minecraft RPG server called DsoCraft. I built custom classes, dungeons, a whole progression system. It had everything — except players. It died not because the game was bad, but because I built it in silence and expected people to find it. That was the lesson. You don't build first and market later. You build an audience and bring them with you.

So this time I'm doing it differently.

Ashen Pantheon is a top-down 2D action RPG. Instanced worlds — your world, your rules, invite up to 3 friends. Three base classes, nine ascendancies you choose permanently at level 50.

I'm one person. I'm studying full time. I have no funding and no team.

But I have the experience of failing once, which is honestly better than never having tried. And this time I'm building in public, from day one.

If this sounds like your kind of game — follow along. It's going to be a long road and I'm not going to pretend otherwise.

Let's go.

— Maks
