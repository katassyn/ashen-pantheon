# Berserker

**Class:** [Warrior](../classes/warrior.md) · **Role:** Melee DPS

> *Pain is fuel. Rage is armor. Death is just another resource.*

The Berserker abandons all pretense of defense and transforms injury into power. Every wound deepens the rage, every point of missing health multiplies the carnage. Berserkers are glass cannons that refuse to break — because they keep stealing life back from everything they hit.

## Core Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Rage Stacking** | Rage builds passively in combat and is consumed by empowered attacks. Higher Rage = higher damage multiplier. |
| **Low HP Scaling** | Ascendancy nodes give flat and percentage damage bonuses that increase as your current HP drops below thresholds (75%, 50%, 25%). |
| **Lifesteal** | A percentage of physical damage dealt is returned as health. Keeps you alive at low HP, enabling the low-HP bonuses. |

## Playstyle

Berserker is a high-risk, high-reward melee damage dealer. You deliberately operate at low health to maximize damage multipliers, relying on lifesteal and burst windows to stay alive. The gameplay loop is: **build Rage → enter low-HP threshold → lifesteal through the burst window → repeat**.

## Ascendancy Tree Focus

Ascendancy tree nodes for Berserker emphasize:

- Increasing Rage generation rate and max stack cap
- Adding more low-HP damage thresholds and stronger multipliers per threshold
- Improving lifesteal rate and adding on-kill healing bursts
- Reducing skill cooldowns while Rage is above a set value

## Synergies

- Gear with **lifesteal %** and **attack speed** amplifies the burst window enormously.
- Movement skills assigned to keybinds let you dash into melee range before Rage decays.
- Avoid stacking too much health — it pushes you away from the low-HP thresholds.
