# Hexblade

**Class:** [Mage](../classes/mage.md) · **Role:** Chaos DoT / Debuffer

> *The curse lands. Then the rot begins. Then you wait.*

The Hexblade is a patient predator. They do not burst enemies down in a flash of lightning — they hex, curse, and corrode them. Hexblades excel at multi-target scenarios, applying devastating damage-over-time effects and auras that make everything weaker for themselves and their party.

## Core Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **AoE Auras** | Persistent fields centered on the Hexblade that apply ongoing effects to enemies within range. Multiple auras can stack. |
| **Curses** | Applied directly to targets. Curses amplify all damage taken by a percentage and can unlock bonus effects for other skills. |
| **Chaos DoT** | Damage-over-time effects that deal chaos (untyped) damage, bypassing elemental resistances entirely. |

## Playstyle

Hexblade is a slow-burn damage dealer. Your opener is always: apply Curse → drop AoE auras → stack DoTs. Once set up, you deal massive sustained damage with minimal input. Against bosses with multiple phases, you must re-apply effects after each phase transition.

## Ascendancy Tree Focus

- Increasing Curse potency and the number of simultaneous Curses per target
- Extending AoE aura radius and adding new aura types
- Boosting Chaos DoT tick rate and damage
- Adding Curse synergy nodes (e.g., cursed targets take bonus lightning damage)
- Unlocking a Hexblade passive that makes a percentage of all damage dealt ignore resistances

## Synergies

- Curses benefit every party member — Hexblade is a strong support pick in 4-player groups.
- Gear should prioritize **DoT damage**, **curse duration**, and **aura effectiveness**.
- Avoid building attack speed — Hexblade's damage comes from setup, not spam.
