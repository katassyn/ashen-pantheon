# Progression

Ashen Pantheon uses a **two-phase progression system** divided at level 50. Each phase has its own skill tree, its own pacing, and its own focus. Reaching level 100 opens endgame content.

## The Full Arc

| Phase | Levels | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | 1 – 49 | Main skill tree, class skills, broad build construction |
| **Ascendancy Choice** | 50 | One permanent ascendancy selected — no going back |
| **Mastery** | 51 – 100 | Ascendancy skill tree, specialization deepens |
| **Endgame** | 100 | Gear farming, challenge bosses, rankings |

## Phase 1: Foundation (Levels 1–49)

You earn **1 skill point per level**, giving you 49 points to spend in the main skill tree by the time you hit the ascendancy gate.

The main skill tree is broad:
- Shared nodes available to all classes
- Class-specific nodes exclusive to your base class
- Many nodes — you will not fill the tree in 49 points; choices matter
- Node types: passive stat boosts, active skill unlocks, and modifier nodes that alter existing skills

See [Skill Tree](skill-tree.md) for full details.

## The Ascendancy Gate (Level 50)

At level 50 your progression pauses until you complete the **Ascendancy Trial** — a solo instance designed to test the playstyle of your chosen ascendancy. On completion, you permanently lock in one of your class's three ascendancy paths.

**This choice cannot be undone.** Choose carefully.

## Phase 2: Mastery (Levels 51–100)

After choosing your ascendancy you enter the second phase. You earn **1 ascendancy point per 2 levels** (levels 52, 54, 56... up to 100), giving you **25 total ascendancy points**.

The ascendancy tree is focused:
- Far fewer nodes than the main tree
- Each node is powerful and identity-defining
- Nodes build on each other — later nodes assume earlier ones are active

See [Ascendancy Tree](ascendancy-tree.md) for full details.

## Endgame (Level 100)

At level 100 the progression systems close and the endgame opens:

- **Gear farming** — hunting specific boss drops to maximize your build
- **Challenge bosses** — harder versions of existing bosses with new mechanics
- **Rankings** — compete for clear speed records and leaderboard positions
