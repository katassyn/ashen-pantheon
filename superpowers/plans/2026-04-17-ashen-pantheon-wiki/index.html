# Game Overview

Ashen Pantheon is set in a fractured realm where the boundaries between mythological pantheons have collapsed. Ancient gods — stripped of their divine order — have gone mad, their power seeping into a world of ash and ruin. You are an **Ashen Hunter**: a mortal who has absorbed enough divine essence to stand against them.

## Core Concept

The game blends responsive top-down action combat with the depth of an ARPG progression system. Every encounter is hand-crafted, every boss tells a story, and every build decision carries weight. The world is yours — but the gods want it back.

## The Instanced World

Each player owns their own **world instance** — a personal version of the game map that only they control. You explore, clear zones, and progress at your own pace without interference from other players.

When you want company, you can invite up to **3 friends** to join your instance, forming a party of 4.

### Global Layer

Even in an instanced world, you're never truly alone. A persistent **global layer** connects all players across instances:

| Layer Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| Chat | Cross-instance channels — general, trade, guild |
| Rankings | Leaderboards for level, boss kills, gear score |
| Trading | Player-to-player item exchange across instances |

## Progression at a Glance

| Phase | Levels | What Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Early Game | 1 – 49 | Main skill tree — broad, many nodes, class skills unlocked |
| Ascendancy | 50 | Choose one ascendancy path (permanent — choose wisely) |
| Mid/Late Game | 51 – 100 | Ascendancy tree — 1 point per 2 levels = 25 total points, fewer but far stronger nodes |
| Endgame | 100 | Gear farming, challenge bosses, rankings |

## The Bosses

Every major boss in Ashen Pantheon is drawn from real-world mythology. Each boss has a unique visual identity, lore connection, and a **multi-phase fight** that escalates in scope and intensity.

**Mythologies represented:**

- **Greek** — Olympian gods and ancient monsters
- **Norse** — Aesir, Vanir, and primordial beasts
- **Egyptian** — Ennead deities and pharaonic guardians
- **Japanese** — Kami and yokai of the spirit world
- **Slavic** — Ancient gods and forest spirits

# Classes

Ashen Pantheon has **three base classes**. Each class shapes your early playstyle and determines which ascendancies you can choose at level 50. The class you pick at character creation is permanent.

| Class | Role | Ascendancies |
|---|---|---|
| [Warrior](warrior.md) | Melee frontline, high HP, physical power | Berserker · Guardian · Paladin |
| [Mage](mage.md) | Ranged caster, elemental mastery, summons | Elementalist · Arcanist · Hexblade |
| [Archer](archer.md) | Ranged precision, mobility, pet synergies | Shadowstalker · Beastmaster · Pathfinder |

## Choosing a Class

Your base class affects:

- **Starting stats** — health, mana, stamina distribution
- **Skill tree shape** — each class has unique skill tree nodes alongside shared nodes
- **Available ascendancies** — you can only pick from your class's three ascendancy paths
- **Weapon proficiency** — Warriors favor melee weapons; Mages favor staves and orbs; Archers favor bows and daggers

# Berserker

**Class:** [Warrior](../classes/warrior.md) · **Role:** Melee DPS

> *Pain is fuel. Rage is armor. Death is just another resource.*

The Berserker abandons all pretense of defense and transforms injury into power. Every wound deepens the rage, every point of missing health multiplies the carnage. Berserkers are glass cannons that refuse to break — because they keep stealing life back from everything they hit.

## Core Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Rage Stacking** | Rage builds passively in combat and is consumed by empowered attacks. Higher Rage = higher damage multiplier. |
| **Low HP Scaling** | Ascendancy nodes give flat and percentage damage bonuses that increase as your current HP drops below thresholds (75%, 50%, 25%). |
| **Lifesteal** | A percentage of physical damage dealt is returned as health. Keeps you alive at low HP, enabling the low-HP bonuses. |

## Playstyle

Berserker is a high-risk, high-reward melee damage dealer. You deliberately operate at low health to maximize damage multipliers, relying on lifesteal and burst windows to stay alive. The gameplay loop is: **build Rage → enter low-HP threshold → lifesteal through the burst window → repeat**.

## Ascendancy Tree Focus

Ascendancy tree nodes for Berserker emphasize:

- Increasing Rage generation rate and max stack cap
- Adding more low-HP damage thresholds and stronger multipliers per threshold
- Improving lifesteal rate and adding on-kill healing bursts
- Reducing skill cooldowns while Rage is above a set value

# Guardian

**Class:** [Warrior](../classes/warrior.md) · **Role:** Tank / Aggro Management

> *The line holds because I hold it.*

The Guardian is the definitive tank of Ashen Pantheon. Where the Berserker bleeds and the Paladin heals, the Guardian does the unglamorous but essential work: drawing every threat, absorbing every hit, and making sure their party never has to.

## Core Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Taunt** | Forces nearby enemies and bosses to redirect their attacks to the Guardian for a duration. Essential for party protection. |
| **Party Shields** | Grants temporary absorption shields to party members. Shields absorb a flat amount of damage before health is affected. |
| **Aggro Management** | Passive nodes ensure the Guardian generates more threat than any other party member, keeping enemies focused on them. |

## Playstyle

Guardian is the only ascendancy built around protecting others. In solo play, you are nearly unkillable but kill slowly. In a party, you are the backbone — your Taunt ability lets allies operate freely, your Party Shields buy crucial seconds, and your aggro management ensures the Mage standing in the back never gets touched.

## Ascendancy Tree Focus

Ascendancy tree nodes for Guardian emphasize:

- Increasing Taunt duration and the number of enemies affected
- Strengthening Party Shield absorption values and cooldown reduction
- Passive threat generation multipliers
- Damage reduction while taunting
- Counter-damage — reflecting a portion of absorbed hits back at attackers

# Paladin

**Class:** [Warrior](../classes/warrior.md) · **Role:** Holy DPS / Party Support

> *Divine fire cleanses what steel cannot.*

The Paladin walks the line between warrior and priest. They deal devastating area-of-effect holy damage, mend wounds with blessed light, and wrap their party in powerful auras that make everyone fight better. The Paladin is the only Warrior ascendancy that meaningfully supports a party, making them one of the most sought-after players in group content.

## Core Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **AoE Holy Damage** | Paladin skills release bursts of holy energy in areas around the caster, dealing magic damage based on the character's holy power stat. |
| **Heals** | Targeted and AoE heals restore health to self and party members. Scales with holy power. |
| **Party Aura Buffs** | Persistent auras that radiate from the Paladin and affect all party members within range — boosting damage, defense, or movement speed. |

## Playstyle

Paladin rewards players who can juggle offensive and supportive responsibilities simultaneously. In boss fights, you are managing your aura selection, throwing out heals when party members take spike damage, and weaving in AoE holy bursts on the boss. You are never standing still.

## Ascendancy Tree Focus

Ascendancy tree nodes for Paladin emphasize:

- Expanding AoE radius and damage of holy burst skills
- Increasing heal potency and adding over-time heal effects
- Unlocking additional aura types (offensive, defensive, utility)
- Reducing aura swap cooldown so you can react to changing fight phases
- Adding holy damage bonuses that scale with current mana

# Elementalist

**Class:** [Mage](../classes/mage.md) · **Role:** Elemental AoE DPS

> *Why choose one element when you can use all three at once?*

The Elementalist is the apex predator of area-of-effect damage. They do not specialize in a single element — they weaponize the reactions between Fire, Ice, and Lightning to create cascading damage chains that can devastate entire boss phases in seconds.

## Core Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Elemental Mixing** | Applying two different elements to the same target creates an elemental reaction (e.g., Fire + Ice = Shatter). Each reaction deals bonus damage and applies a status effect. |
| **AoE Bursts** | Elementalist signature skills release large area bursts of a chosen element, letting you apply elements to entire groups simultaneously. |
| **Elemental Mastery** | Passive stat that increases the damage and effect of all elemental reactions triggered. |

## Elemental Reactions

| Element A | Element B | Reaction | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fire | Ice | Shatter | Massive burst damage + freeze |
| Fire | Lightning | Overload | Chain lightning to nearby enemies |
| Ice | Lightning | Superconductor | AoE slow + defense reduction |

# Arcanist

**Class:** [Mage](../classes/mage.md) · **Role:** Elemental Summoner / Support

> *Why fight when your creations can fight for you?*

The Arcanist forges living constructs from elemental energy and sends them into battle. Part summoner, part strategist, the Arcanist's power scales with how well you manage your elemental entities — positioning them, directing their abilities, and keeping them alive long enough to matter.

## Core Mechanics

# Progression

Ashen Pantheon uses a **two-phase progression system** divided at level 50. Each phase has its own skill tree, its own pacing, and its own focus. Reaching level 100 opens endgame content.

## The Full Arc

| Phase | Levels | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | 1 – 49 | Main skill tree, class skills, broad build construction |
| **Ascendancy Choice** | 50 | One permanent ascendancy selected — no going back |
| **Mastery** | 51 – 100 | Ascendancy skill tree, specialization deepens |
| **Endgame** | 100 | Gear farming, challenge bosses, rankings |

## Phase 1: Foundation (Levels 1–49)

You earn **1 skill point per level**, giving you 49 points to spend in the main skill tree by the time you hit the ascendancy gate.

The main skill tree is broad:
- Shared nodes available to all classes
- Class-specific nodes exclusive to your base class
- Many nodes — you will not fill the tree in 49 points; choices matter
- Node types: passive stat boosts, active skill unlocks, and modifier nodes that alter existing skills

See [Skill Tree](skill-tree.md) for full details.

## The Ascendancy Gate (Level 50)

At level 50 your progression pauses until you complete the **Ascendancy Trial** — a solo instance designed to test the playstyle of your chosen ascendancy. On completion, you permanently lock in one of your class's three ascendancy paths.

**This choice cannot be undone.** Choose carefully.

## Phase 2: Mastery (Levels 51–100)

After choosing your ascendancy you enter the second phase. You earn **1 ascendancy point per 2 levels** (levels 52, 54, 56... up to 100), giving you **25 total ascendancy points**.

The ascendancy tree is focused:
- Far fewer nodes than the main tree
- Each node is powerful and identity-defining
- Nodes build on each other — later nodes assume earlier ones are active

See [Ascendancy Tree](ascendancy-tree.md) for full details.

## Endgame (Level 100)

At level 100 the progression systems close and the endgame opens:

- **Gear farming** — hunting specific boss drops to maximize your build
- **Challenge bosses** — harder versions of existing bosses with new mechanics
- **Rankings** — compete for clear speed records and leaderboard positions

# Skill Tree (Levels 1–50)

The main skill tree is the foundation of every build in Ashen Pantheon. It is a large, branching network of nodes that you navigate from level 1 through level 49, spending one point per level.

## Overview

- **49 points** available (one per level, levels 1–49)
- Tree is **shared across classes** at the outer edges, with **class-exclusive sections** in the center
- Node types: passive stats, active skill unlocks, and skill modifiers
- You cannot fill the entire tree — builds are defined by what you choose to skip

## Node Types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Passive Stat Node** | Directly increases a stat (e.g., +5% physical damage, +20 max health) |
| **Skill Unlock Node** | Adds a new active skill to your available skill pool |
| **Modifier Node** | Changes the behavior of a specific skill (e.g., "Fire Ball now hits twice but costs 20% more mana") |
| **Keystone Node** | Rare, powerful nodes that fundamentally change how a mechanic works. Located deep in the tree. |

## Class Sections

Each class has an exclusive section of the tree that only they can access:

| Class | Tree Section Focus |
|---|---|
| Warrior | Melee damage, armor scaling, stance nodes |
| Mage | Elemental affinity, cast speed, mana efficiency |
| Archer | Range multipliers, mobility skills, pet slot nodes |

## Build Philosophy

The skill tree rewards specialization. Spreading points evenly produces a weak generalist build. Strong builds commit to a clear path:

1. Rush toward key Skill Unlock nodes early
2. Fill in passive stat nodes along the path
3. Pick up Modifier nodes for your core active skills
4. Consider Keystone nodes as long-term goals

# Ascendancy Tree (Levels 50–100)

The ascendancy tree is the second phase of character progression, unlocked after you choose your ascendancy at level 50. It is a smaller, more powerful tree exclusive to your chosen ascendancy path.

## Overview

- **25 points** available (one per 2 levels from 52 to 100)
- Tree is **exclusive to your ascendancy** — Berserker and Guardian trees are completely different
- Nodes are fewer but far more impactful than main skill tree nodes
- Tree is designed to amplify and complete the build direction started in the main tree

| Level Range | Points Earned | Cumulative Total |
|---|---|---|
| 50 | 0 (tree unlocked, no points yet) | 0 |
| 52 | 1 | 1 |
| 54 | 1 | 2 |
| ... | ... | ... |
| 100 | 1 | 25 |

Ascendancy nodes are intentionally strong. Examples of the tier of power:

- "Your critical strikes deal 40% more damage"
- "Lifesteal heals for double the amount when below 50% health"

These are build-defining, not incremental.

The ascendancy tree assumes you have built the main tree to support it. For example, Berserker ascendancy nodes that scale with Rage assume you have unlocked Rage-generating skills in the main tree.

Plan your main tree with your intended ascendancy in mind from character creation.

# Skill Slots

Ashen Pantheon uses a **free keybind system** — any skill you have unlocked can be assigned to any keybind slot, with no restrictions.

## How It Works

1. **Unlock skills** via the skill tree (Skill Unlock nodes) and ascendancy tree
2. Open the **Skills panel** to see all unlocked skills
3. **Drag any skill** to any empty keybind slot
4. Your keybind is now active in combat

## No Restrictions

There is no concept of a "skill type" that limits assignment. You can:

- Fill every slot with damage skills
- Mix damage, defense, and movement skills freely
- Assign healing skills alongside crowd-control skills
- Run zero defensive skills if you prefer maximum aggression (not recommended)

This means the optimal skill layout is a meaningful build decision, not a default.

# Instancing

Ashen Pantheon uses a **player-owned instancing system**. Every player controls their own copy of the game world, with the option to invite others.

## Your World Instance

When you log in, you enter your personal instance — a version of the game world that belongs exclusively to you. You control:

- **Progression state** — which bosses you have killed, which zones are cleared
- **Access** — who can enter your world
- **Pace** — there is no server-wide day cycle or shared resource depletion

Nobody can enter your instance without your invitation.

## Party System

You can invite up to **3 other players** to your instance, creating a party of 4.

| Action | How |
|---|---|
| Invite a player | Search by name in the Social panel → Send Instance Invite |
| Accept an invite | Notification appears → Accept to join their instance |
| Leave a party | Party panel → Leave Instance (you return to your own world) |
| Kick a party member | Party leader only — Party panel → Kick Player |

When a party member enters your instance, their progression in **your** world does not affect their own instance. Boss kills in your instance count for you; you must enter their instance for them to get credit.

## Global Layer

Even inside your personal instance, a **global layer** connects you to all players:

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Global Chat** | Persistent text channels shared by all players regardless of instance |
| **Rankings** | Live leaderboards for level, boss kills, and gear score — updated in real time |
| **Trading** | List items for sale and purchase items from other players across instances |

The global layer ensures the game feels like a living world even within solo instancing.

## Boss Instance Scaling

Boss encounters scale based on party size:

| Party Size | Boss HP Multiplier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1× | Solo tuning — designed to be clearable |
| 2 | 1.6× | Slight increase |
| 3 | 2.1× | Moderate increase |
| 4 | 2.5× | Full party tuning |

# Boss Phases

All major bosses in Ashen Pantheon use a **multi-phase fight system**. As a boss's health drops, the encounter changes — new mechanics appear, existing ones become more dangerous, and the visual presentation escalates.

## Why Phases Matter

A single-phase boss fight is a test of one skill set. A multi-phase boss fight is a test of adaptability. Ashen Pantheon bosses are designed so that the strategy that works at 100% health does not simply work at 20% health — you must adjust.

## Standard Phase Structure

Most bosses use a three-phase structure, though this varies:

| Phase | HP Threshold | Typical Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | 100% – 66% | Core moveset, learning phase, moderate difficulty |
| Phase 2 | 66% – 33% | New abilities added, arena may change, damage increases |
| Phase 3 | 33% – 0% | Enraged state, highest damage, signature ultimate ability |

## Phase Transitions

Phase transitions are dramatic, scripted moments:

- Boss becomes temporarily invulnerable
- A cutscene or animation plays showing the boss's escalation
- The arena may change (new hazards appear, geometry shifts)
- The boss resets any stacks or statuses

Phase transitions are the safest moment to use consumables and communicate with your party.

## Enrage Timers

Some bosses have a soft enrage — if you do not reach a phase threshold within a time limit, the boss gains a permanent damage buff. This prevents excessively slow fights.

Many bosses have mechanics unique to a single phase:

- A Greek boss might summon divine champions only in Phase 2
- A Norse boss might trigger Ragnarok effects in Phase 3, filling the arena with rising water
- An Egyptian boss might require you to solve a puzzle during the Phase 1→2 transition

# #001 — The Beginning

*April 17, 2026*

---

Every project starts somewhere. This one started with a frustration.

I've played a lot of action RPGs. I love the genre deeply — the satisfying click of a build coming together, the moment a boss's pattern finally clicks, the way a well-designed skill tree can hold your attention for hundreds of hours. But I kept running into the same walls: instancing systems that felt like afterthoughts, boss fights that were just stat checks dressed up in flashy animations, and ascendancy systems that felt like the designers ran out of budget halfway through.

So I started designing the game I wanted to play.

## What Ashen Pantheon Is

At its core, it's a **top-down 2D action RPG** — responsive, fast, skill-expressive. The camera sits above the character. Combat is immediate. Every fight is something you actively participate in, not something you watch your character do.

The instancing is first-class. Your world is yours. That means no competition for resources, no one ruining your boss attempt, no waiting for a respawn. But it doesn't mean isolation — the global layer keeps you connected to everyone else: chat is shared, rankings are live, trading works across instances.

The bosses are mythological. Not loosely inspired — actually pulled from real pantheons. Greek, Norse, Egyptian, Japanese, Slavic. I've been doing a lot of reading.

## The Three Classes

Three base classes: **Warrior**, **Mage**, **Archer**. Clean archetypes, but with room to go strange. Each branches into three ascendancies at level 50 — nine total paths, each with a distinct mechanical identity. The Hexblade is a chaos-DoT mage who curses targets and watches them rot. The Shadowstalker is an archer who closes to melee range and backstabs. The Pathfinder is a tank archer who wins by outlasting everything.

## The Two-Phase Skill System

Levels 1–50: broad skill tree, many nodes, class-specific paths, deep customization. Levels 50–100: focused ascendancy tree, 25 powerful points, 1 per 2 levels. The inflection at 50 is the most important decision in the game.

## Why This Wiki

I'm building this wiki as a public development document — it will grow as the game grows. Pages marked **[TODO]** aren't placeholders forever; they're honest signals about where development hasn't reached yet. When a skill tree gets designed, the wiki gets updated. When a boss gets named, the wiki gets its page.

This is a living document.
